**Data annotation F11a German:**

**General remarks:**

1.  The study was automatically transcribed, translated, and glossed by
    an app.

2.  The automatic transcription was manually corrected by a German
    native speaker who was a graduate student of German studies. The
    same student assistant picked out the relevant utterances for
    glossing/coding. (Sandra)

3.  The automatic translation was corrected by a German native speaker
    who was an undergraduate student of English studies. (Celia)

4.  The glossing was first done manually and then corrected by the same
    student assistant as the translation. (Celia)

5.  12% of the transcribed, translated, and glossed data was
    cross-checked. (Emilia)

6.  The general transcription, translation and glossing conventions can
    be found on Github
    (<https://github.com/LeibnizDream/A02-Instructions/tree/main/Data_Handling_Process)>

**Transcription and audio files:**

1.  Transcriptions include any utterances made by all parties present,
    including participants, experperimenter or any accompanying third
    parties.

2.  The transcriptions follow the general conventions of the
    LeibnizDream project which are based on the CHAT manual from the
    CHILDES webpage
    (<https://github.com/LeibnizDream/A02-Instructions/blob/main/Data_Handling_Process/transcription_conventions.md)>

3.  We strictly adhere to the project's ethics and keep the participants
    anonymous, codifying any personal identifiable information (PII).

4.  Relevant transcription decisions

    a.  Interjections (all marked \@i): '\'hä\' = \'huh\', \'mhm\' =
        \'mhm\', \'mhmhmh\' = \'mhmhmh\', \'hm\' = \'hm\', \'hmhm\' =
        \'hmhm\', \'öhm\' = \'uhm\', 'öh' = 'uh', 'äh' = 'uh'

    b.  Non-adultlike forms were marked \@g and transcribed as produced.
        Additionally, the standard version was inferred when possible
        and noted in brackets: e.g. *die gnüne \[:grüne\] katze*

**Translation:**

The translation is based on the German corrected transcription and
integrates non-adult like forms as well as their inferred standard
versions. For a word-for-word translation of relevant utterances, refer
to glossing.

**Glossing:**

1.  Glossing was only done for participants' relevant utterances in
    accordance with the Leipzig glossing rules:
    <https://www.eva.mpg.de/lingua/resources/glossing-rules.php>

2.  General Glossing Decisions: In general, minimal glossing was
    applied, meaning that only features marked by distinct morphemes
    were glossed. However, for negative indefinites, determiners as well
    as demonstrative pronouns, a maximal glossing strategy was applied.
    Comments in the glossing_comment column mark any
    mismatches/deviation from adult-like grammar.

Morphosegmentation: The utterances were morphologically segmented before
glossing in the following way:

i.  Morpheme borders were marked with "X-Y"

ii. Circumfixes were marked "X\>Y\<CIRC"

iii. No vowel alternations were marked on the stem

iv. **Examples:**

v.  \(i\) hase-n / rabbit-PL

vi. \(ii\) ge\>rutsch\<t / PASTP\>slide\<CIRC

vii. \(iii\) sang / sing\\PST

3.  Specific Glossing Decisions

    i.  **CASE**:

- ambiguities due to syncretism in the German case system were only
  glossed in incomplete sentences or in the case of mismatches between
  determiner and noun otherwise they were inferred from the question
  context

- Mismatches on case were mentioned in the commments column, including
  grammatical correct case

> **Examples:**

- der schmetterling hat mit den radiergummi ge\>spiel\<t /
  DET.DEF.M.SG.NOM butterfly have.3SG with DET.DEF.M.SG.ACC eraser
  PASTP\>play\<CIRC + comment: case mismatch between DEF.DEF.ACC and
  with DET.DEF.DAT (requires DAT)

<!-- -->

- die pinsel-n ge\>nomm\<en / DET.DEF.PL.ACC brush-PL.DAT
  PASTP\>take\<CIRC + comment: case-mismatch between DET.DEF.M.PL-DAT
  and DET.DEF.M.PL.ACC (requires ACC)

  i.  **Number:**

<!-- -->

- Number indicated on verbs

- Number indicated on demonstrative pronouns

> **Examples**:

- die grüne kuh hat / DET.DEF.F.SG.NOM green-AGR cow have.3SG

<!-- -->

- Weiß ich nicht / know.1SG I NEG

  i.  **Gender**:

<!-- -->

- Same minimal glossing stategy as before

- no gender glossed for plural forms unless it's marked by a distinct
  morpheme

> **Examples:**

- No gender for plural gloss: die pinsel / *DET.DEF.PL.ACC brush*

<!-- -->

- Gender mismatch: *das kleber und das normal-e stift / DET.DEF.N.SG.ACC
  glue and DET.DEF.N.SG.ACC normal-AGR pen*

  i.  **DP agreement:**

<!-- -->

- full glossing only on determiner

- -AGR on overt suffixes (nouns, adjectives)

- no marking of CNG (Case, Number, Gender) on nouns

> **Examples**:
>
> *ein grün-er ball / DET.INDF.M.SG.NOM green-AGR ball*
>
> **Motivation**: In German DPs, determiner, (adjective) and noun
> exhibit concord in case, number and gender. In the glosses, we
> indicate case, number, and gender on the determiner only. On
> adjectives, we only gloss overt suffixes as -AGR. On nouns there is no
> marking at all, which corresponds to minimal glossing for everything
> except Negative Indefinites.

i.  **Verbs:**

- for present tense forms, no tense, only person and number were marked

- for past tense forms, past was marked

- participles were marked as PASTP\>\<CIRC

> **Examples:**
>
> *kürbis ge\>gess\<t \--\> pumpkin PASTP\>eat\<CIRC*
>
> weiß Ich nicht / know.1SG I NEG

i.  **Nominalizations:** marked as Verb-gender.number.case

ii. **Particle verbs**:

- for German particle verbs, particles were translated to English; for
  prefix verbs, the entire prefix + verb complex was translated

> **Examples:**
>
> *an-fang-en / at-start-INF; auf-hör-en / off-stop-INF*
>
> ***vs.** versteck-en / hide-INF*

i.  **Negative indefinites**

- Differentiation between German kein(e/r) and nichts/niemand, indicated
  with k or n

- In case of "gar nichts", gar marked as intensifier

- Keiner & niemand glossed with case

- When gender, number, case have special morpheme ending to NEG.INDF,
  added with -- gender, number, case

- Difference between animate & inanimate, when inanimate: INAN & for
  animate: AN

> **Examples:**
>
> gar nichts / INTS n.NEG.INDF.INAN
>
> Nichts / n.NEG.INDF.INAN
>
> Nix / n.NEG.INDF.INAN
>
> Niemand / n.NEG.INDF.AN.NOM
>
> Kein / k.NEG.INDF
>
> kein-er / k.NEG.INDF.-M.SG.NOM

4.  Abbreviations used:

    a.  Categories: DET = determiner; PRO = pronoun; DEM = demonstrative
        (pronoun); REL = relative (pronoun)

    b.  Case: NOM = nominative; ACC = accusative; DAT = dative; GEN =
        genitive

    c.  Number: SG = singular; PL = plural

    d.  Person: 1 = first person; 2 = second; 3 = third

    e.  Gender: M = masculine; F = feminine; N = neuter

    f.  Tense: PST = past

    g.  Participles: PASTP= past participle

    h.  Others: INF = infinitive; DEF = definite; INDF = indefinite; NEG
        = negation; INTS = intensifier; k=German keiner; n=nichts; AN =
        animate; INAN = inanimate
